Lenny for Libraries

Lenny: The Library-in-a-Box

Lenny is a plug-and-play, open source, Library-in-a-Box that allows libraries to preserve and lend digital books.

Try Lenny

A Way to Preserve Digital Books

Today, many libraries are only given an option to rent ebooks, from platforms like Overdrive.

  • It's unsustainable for libraries to re-pay every year to rent the same ebooks.
  • When libraries rent, they don't own any books and can't preserve an equitable collection.
  • Many libraries don't have an alternative to buy, preserve, or lend digital books.

Here's the vision we aim to build — together...

Build your Library

  • Lenny comes preloaded with thousands of open access titles
  • Easily import & secure your library books
  • Buy & import from openlibrary.press with 1-click

Lend your books directly to your patrons

  • Customizable digital lending rules

Automatic up-to-date OPDS Feeds

A federated future for libraries

Imagine an world where every library is a critical destination in a unified, global network:

  • Lenny super-charges every library into a digitally sovereign, discoverable partner.
  • OpenLibrary.org helps us connect: empowering 11.5M million readers to find and borrow books directly from participating libraries.
  • OpenLibrary.press empowers authors and publishers to escape the walls of corporate platforms and securely sell DRM-free books.

Built to Ensure Library Futures

Lenny is the missing infrastructure libraries need to reassert ownership over digital books. Paired with OpenLibrary.press, libraries can now buy ownable books directly from authors and host them locally or collaboratively.

Discovery happens through OpenLibrary.org, which connects millions of readers to books hosted by libraries, returning traffic and trust to the public web.

With Our Powers Combined:

When you combine Lenny, OpenLibrary.org, OpenLibrary.press, you don't get a platform, you get a public utility for books. Lenny helps libraries retain their role as digital stewards of knowledge, while OpenLibrary.org powers discovery and access across the so patrons always find their next great read.

Self-Hosted

Run Lenny as a Docker container on your own server. Full control, full flexibility, zero vendor lock-in.

  • Open source & customizable
  • Runs on standard Linux infrastructure
  • Integrated with Open Library metadata

Plug & Play

Get Lenny pre-installed on a Raspberry plug it in and start lending digital books securely.

  • No setup required
  • Offline-ready local lending
  • Simple updates & connectivity

Hosted

Let us take care of everything. A hosted Lenny instance with a web dashboard for simple, secure access.

  • Zero maintenance
  • Web-based admin tools
  • Scalable and secure infrastructure

Why Lenny?

  • Self-hosted: Open source Docker-powered server you control
  • Plug & Play: Delivered ready-to-run on Raspberry Pi hardware
  • Hosted: Access via a clean web interface with zero setup
  • Secure Lending: Built-in LCP support + Thorium Web Reader
  • Integrated Metadata: Always up-to-date via OpenLibrary.org
  • Mobile Ready: OPDS feed works with Thorium, SimplyE, Aldiko

Free, Open Source, Built by Nonprofits.

Lenny is an open prototype, inclubated by Archive Labs and the team behind Open Library at the Internet Archive. It's designed as a public good: a free digital circulation system for libraries that want to preserve, lend, and serve their communities on their own terms.
